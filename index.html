LENTEN PRAYERS
Prayer of St. Ephraim
+ Prayer Before Holy Scripture
Prayer Before Reading Holy Scripture
📖 SAY THIS BEFORE YOUR DAILY READINGS
Illumine our hearts, O Master Who lovest mankind,
with the pure light of Thy divine knowledge.

Open the eyes of our mind to the understanding
of Thy gospel teachings.

Implant also in us the fear of Thy blessed commandments,
that trampling down all carnal desires,
we may enter upon a spiritual manner of living,
both thinking and doing such things as are well-pleasing unto Thee.

For Thou art the illumination of our souls and bodies,
O Christ our God,
and unto Thee we ascribe glory,
together with Thy Father, Who is from everlasting,
and Thine all-holy, good, and life-creating Spirit,
now and ever and unto ages of ages. Amen.
Prayer of St. Ephraim — Full Text with Prostrations
Said twice at every Lenten weekday service (Mon–Fri). Three prostrations + twelve bows.
O Lord and Master of my life!

Take from me the spirit of sloth,
despondency, lust of power,
and idle talk.
🙇 FULL PROSTRATION (sign of Cross → bow to floor)
But give rather the spirit of chastity,
humility, patience,
and love to Thy servant.
🙇 FULL PROSTRATION
Yea, O Lord and King!
Grant me to see my own errors
and not to judge my brother,
for Thou art blessed unto ages of ages. Amen.
🙇 FULL PROSTRATION
Then 12 bows from the waist, saying at each:
"O God, cleanse me a sinner."

Then repeat the entire prayer once more → one final prostration.
Who Was St. Ephraim?
St. Ephraim of Nisibis (c. 306–373 AD) 🌿
A deacon, theologian, and poet of the 4th-century Syrian Church — called the "Harp of the Holy Spirit." He wrote thousands of hymns in Syriac. His prayer became the central Lenten prayer of the entire Orthodox Church, prayed at every weekday service from Cheesefare Week through Holy Wednesday. Feast: Jan 28 (Julian) / Feb 10 (Gregorian).
The Four Vices We Ask to Lose
😴 Sloth — Root of All Sin
Not just laziness but spiritual paralysis — the cynicism that says "what's the point?" It poisons the will to repent. Fr. Schmemann: "the poisoning of spiritual energy at its very source."
😔 Despondency — Despair About Mercy
Convinces us our sins are too great, that repentance is useless. The Prodigal Son story is the antidote: the Father always runs to meet us.
👑 Lust of Power — Domination of Others
The desire to control, to win, to impose our will. The antidote is the Cross: "Whoever wishes to be first shall be your slave" — Christ washed feet.
💬 Idle Talk — Scattered, Empty Speech
Gossip, complaints, speech without substance. The Desert Fathers: "Sit in your cell and your cell will teach you everything." Silence is a form of fasting.
The Four Virtues We Ask to Receive
🧠 Chastity — Whole-mindedness
The Greek sophrosyne: soundness and wholeness of mind. Directing all energy toward God rather than scattering it in distraction and dissipation.
🙏 Humility — Seeing Ourselves Truthfully
Not self-deprecation but truth: seeing ourselves as we are before God. "God, be merciful to me, a sinner." The doorway through which all grace enters.
⏳ Patience — Long-suffering Without Bitterness
The capacity to endure difficulty without despair. Transforms pain into prayer. The fast itself is a school of patience: the body learns to wait, so the soul learns to trust.
❤️ Love — The Crown of All Virtues
Self-giving agape, not sentiment. All Lenten discipline — prayer, fasting, almsgiving — exists only to make this possible. "By this shall all men know that you are My disciples."
How to Use Both Prayers at Home
Before reading: Say the Prayer Before Holy Scripture first.
Step 1: Stand before your icons. Sign of the Cross.
Step 2: Part 1 of St. Ephraim → full prostration.
Step 3: Part 2 → full prostration.
Step 4: Part 3 → full prostration.
Step 5: 12 waist bows: "O God, cleanse me a sinner."
Step 6: Repeat the whole prayer once more → final prostration.

Mon–Fri only. Not on Saturdays, Sundays, or Great Feasts.
DAILY DISCIPLINE
Lenten Routine
A suggested daily rhythm for Great Lent
☀️ Morning — Upon Waking
🌅
Morning Prayers (10–15 min)
Begin with the Trisagion, then the morning prayer rule from your prayer book. Ask a blessing on the day.
🙏 Prayer
✝️
Prayer of St. Ephraim (5–10 min)
3 sections, 3 prostrations, 12 bows, then repeated. The heart of the Lenten rule. Done at the start and end of each day.
🙇 Prostrations
📖
Prayer Before Scripture + Daily Reading
Say the Prayer Before Reading Holy Scripture, then read today's assigned passages. Check them off in the Calendar tab when done.
📖 Scripture
🍵
Break Fast — Simple, Late, Mindful
Mon/Wed/Fri: one meal in the evening ideally. Tue/Thu: oil and wine permitted. Fast with purpose, not just habit.
🍽️ Fasting
⏸️ Midday — Work & World
🔁
Jesus Prayer — Throughout the Day
"Lord Jesus Christ, Son of God, have mercy on me, a sinner." Repeat as you walk, drive, wait. The prayer of the heart carried through the day.
🙏 Prayer
🤐
Fast from Idle Talk
Reduce scrolling, entertainment, gossip. 30 min of intentional quiet changes the inner climate. The Desert Fathers: silence is the mother of prayer.
⚡ Discipline
🤲
Almsgiving — The Third Pillar
Each week: give something — money, time, a call to someone lonely, a meal. Do it secretly. Prayer, fasting, and almsgiving form the three pillars of Lenten repentance.
❤️ Alms
🕯️ Evening — Return to God
⛪
Church Services — When Possible
Wed & Fri evenings: Liturgy of the Presanctified Gifts — the great Lenten service. Come fasting from morning. Other evenings: Vespers and Compline.
⛪ Church
📚
Spiritual Reading (15–30 min)
The Ladder of Divine Ascent · Great Lent (Fr. Schmemann) · Life of St. Mary of Egypt · The Psalter · Lives of Saints.
📖 Reading
🌙
Evening Prayers + Examination (10–15 min)
Evening prayer rule. Where did sloth, despondency, power-seeking, or idle talk win today? Ask forgiveness. Then rest in peace.
🙏 Prayer
✝️
Prayer of St. Ephraim (evening repeat)
Repeat with prostrations. End your day as you began — on your knees, turning from self to God.
🙇 Prostrations
📅 Weekly Pattern
Monday: Renewal. Fresh start. Xerophagy.
Tuesday: Oil & wine permitted.
Wednesday: Strict fast. ★ Presanctified Liturgy tonight.
Thursday: Oil & wine. Midweek spiritual reading.
Friday: Strict fast. ★ Presanctified / Akathist.
Saturday: No prostrations. Fish & oil permitted. Souls Saturdays = memorial.
Sunday: 🌟 Little Pascha. Fish, wine & oil. Rejoice!
